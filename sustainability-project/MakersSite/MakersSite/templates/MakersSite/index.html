Philosphy Behind Things

Makers, Hackers, and tinkerers: These people focus on the Hands on approach of things, it is less important to really know why things are. It is more important to know how things work. I personally will only go half way through a tutorial inorder to get through the basics. Then I start messing around with the tools I know and if I need to know anything beyond the basics I will look it up. I typically forget how it works, so multiple lookups makes me momorize the key concepts. That's not the best way of learning concepts, but it is the most fun way.

However, in professional and more serious contexts, it is better to read up and sit and think for several hours, then to get to Hacking. Hacking mindsets makes writing comments break flow and makes writing comments a very boring thing.
